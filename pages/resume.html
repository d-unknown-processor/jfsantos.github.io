Resume

Education

  • 2013 - ongoing: Ph.D. in Telecommunications at INRS-EMT (Montréal, QC, Canada)

    Research topic: quality- and environment-aware speech enhancement.

  • 2012 - 2013: M. Sc. in Telecommunications at INRS-EMT (Montréal, QC, Canada)

    MSc Thesis Title: A non-intrusive objective speech intelligibility metric tailored for cochlear implant users in complex listening environments.

  • 2005 - 2011: Electrical Engineering at Universidade Federal de Santa Catarina (Florianópolis, SC, Brazil)

    Undergraduate senior year thesis: A reconfigurable parallel architecture for baseband processing in software-defined radios.

Research experience

  • 2012 - ongoing: Graduate Researcher at MuSAE Lab - INRS (Montréal, QC, Canada)

    Research Topic: Quality-aware speech dereverberation and denoising.

    Abstract: This project is aimed at developing quality- and environment-aware speech enhancement algorithms for next-generation speech applications. This project has as partners the Communications Research Centre Canada and a leading Canadian hearing aid company.

  • 2011 - 2012: Research & Development Engineer at Acústica Amplivox Ltda (Florianópolis, SC, Brazil)

    Research Topic: Hearing aid design.

    Abstract: This was the first digital hearing aid developed by a Brazilian company. Project included software, firmware, system level design and DSP algorithms for hearing aid devices.

  • 2010: Undergraduate Researcher at Software/Hardware Integration Laboratory - UFSC (Florianópolis, SC, Brazil)

    Research Topic: Embedded Software-Defined Radio (eSDR).

    Abstract: The eSDR Project aimed to develop an SDR platform independent of a host computer. This project was a partnership between LISHA and Digitro, a Brazilian company that develops specialized telecom appliances. The team worked on research and development of the DSP system design and the software/hardware architecture for the platform.

  • 2007 - 2009: Undergraduate research intern at Acústica Amplivox Ltda (Florianópolis, SC, Brazil)

    Research Topic: Development of a configuration system for hearing aid devices.

    Abstract: During this internship, a configuration system for hearing aid devices was developed. All design steps, from parameter computation to user interface design, were explored.

  • 2006 - 2007: Undergraduate Researcher at Automation and Systems Department - UFSC (Florianópolis, SC, Brazil)

    Research Topic: Exploiting tuple spaces to provide fault-tolerant scheduling on computational grids.

    Abstract: This project explored a tuple space based approach to develop a scheduling heuristic for applications with decoupled tasks on computational grids. A simulator was developed to compare this heuristic with other approaches.
